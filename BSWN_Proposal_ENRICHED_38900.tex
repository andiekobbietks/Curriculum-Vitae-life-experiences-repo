\section{Understanding the UnMuseum Vision}

The UnMuseum is not simply a digital archive but a \textbf{Living Archive}: a relational platform that centres Black Bristolian lived experiences rather than colonial taxonomies.

\subsection{Decolonial Archiving Approach}

Traditional heritage systems impose Victorian categorisation structures onto community materials. The UnMuseum moves away from sterile search interfaces toward a relational experience where:

\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=*]
    \item \textbf{Stories navigate to other stories} through emotional, thematic, and geographic connections
    \item \textbf{Community members co-curate} rather than passive consumption
    \item \textbf{Dublin Core metadata} supports interoperability without imposing hierarchy
    \item \textbf{WCAG 2.1 AA compliance} ensures accessibility for all community members
\end{itemize}

\subsection{What is SFIA?}

The \textbf{Skills Framework for the Information Age (SFIA)} is the globally accepted standard for describing and managing IT skills and competencies. It is used by:

\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=*]
    \item \textbf{UK Government Digital Service} for all public sector IT procurement
    \item \textbf{G-Cloud Framework} (Crown Commercial Service) for rate benchmarking
    \item \textbf{NHS Digital}, \textbf{Ministry of Defence}, and major public bodies
    \item Over 200 organisations in 180 countries worldwide
\end{itemize}

SFIA defines seven levels of responsibility, from Level 1 (Follow) to Level 7 (Set Strategy). Each level has clear descriptors for autonomy, influence, complexity, and business skills.

\subsubsection{SFIA Level Definitions}

\begin{table}[h]
\centering
\rowcolors{2}{lightgrey}{white}
\begin{tabular}{C{1.5cm}L{3cm}L{8.5cm}}
\toprule
\textbf{\textcolor{HeritageGreen}{Level}} & \textbf{\textcolor{HeritageGreen}{Name}} & \textbf{\textcolor{HeritageGreen}{Description}} \\
\midrule
Level 3 & Apply & Works under general supervision, uses discretion in identifying and resolving problems \\
Level 4 & Enable & Works under general direction, substantial personal responsibility, influences team decisions \\
Level 5 & Ensure/Advise & Broad direction, full accountability for technical decisions, advises on scope of work \\
Level 6 & Initiate/Influence & Defined authority at organisational level, establishes organisational objectives \\
\bottomrule
\end{tabular}
\caption{SFIA Level Definitions (Levels 3 to 6)}
\end{table}

\subsubsection{Why SFIA Matters for BSWN}

Using SFIA-based pricing ensures:

\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=*]
    \item \textbf{Transparency}: Rates are benchmarked against published government frameworks
    \item \textbf{Audit trail}: National Lottery Heritage Fund can verify rates against G-Cloud comparables
    \item \textbf{Fair value}: Neither overpaying nor underpaying for the skill level required
    \item \textbf{Accountability}: Clear mapping between skills and deliverables
\end{itemize}
